# Results

This folder contains all output plots from the 2³ Full Factorial DoE analysis.

---

## 1. pareto_plot.png

**What it shows:**
Ranks all effects (N, P, K and interactions) by their absolute size.

**How to read it:**
- Longer bar = stronger effect on soil pH
- Tells you which factor matters most

---

## 2. main_effects_plot.png

**What it shows:**
How soil pH changes when each factor moves from Low (−1) to High (+1).

**How to read it:**
- Steep slope = strong main effect
- Flat line = factor has little effect on pH
- Upward slope = higher level increases pH
- Downward slope = higher level decreases pH

---

## 3. interaction_plots.png

**What it shows:**
Three plots — N×P, N×K, P×K interactions.

**How to read it:**
- Parallel lines = no interaction between factors
- Crossing or diverging lines = significant interaction exists
- Interaction means the effect of one factor depends on the level of another

---

## 4. cube_plot.png

**What it shows:**
A 3D cube where each corner represents one of the 8 treatment combinations, labeled with the mean soil pH value.

**How to read it:**
- Each corner = one experimental run
- Compare opposite corners to see factor effects visually
- Higher pH values = more alkaline soil conditions

---

## 5. residual_plots.png

**What it shows:**
Two diagnostic plots to validate model assumptions.

| Plot | What it Checks |
|---|---|
| **Normal Probability Plot** | Are residuals normally distributed? Points should follow a straight line |
| **Residuals vs Fitted** | Is variance constant? Points should scatter randomly around zero |

**How to read it:**
- Points close to line in QQ plot = normality assumption satisfied 
- Random scatter around zero = model is valid 
- Patterns or curves = model assumptions may be violated
